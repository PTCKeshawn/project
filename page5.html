What Actions Have we taken So far to Take Care Of Global Warming?

  • The 1979 World Climate Conference was the first major global meeting on climate change. It urged nations “to foresee and to prevent potential man-made changes in climate that might be adverse to the well-being of humanity.”

  • In 1988, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) was formed to study human-caused climate change and suggest possible solutions.

  • The Kyoto Protocol was adopted in 1997. It focused on reducing six major greenhouse gases, including carbon dioxide and methane.

    • The United States, although a major emitter, rejected the agreement because it felt the protocol's rules would hurt the economy and were unfair for not including developing countries.

    • In 2005, the protocol officially went into effect after Russia approved it, meeting the required threshold for ratification.

  • Public awareness increased with the 2006 release of An Inconvenient Truth by Al Gore, and the documentary based on it helped spread knowledge about the dangers of climate change.

  • In 2007, Al Gore and the IPCC won the Nobel Peace Prize for “efforts to build up and disseminate greater knowledge about man-made climate change.”

* Al Gore and IPCC recieving Nobel Peace Prize for efforts to spread awareness on global warming.
