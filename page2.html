Who Thought of these Ideas? : The Industrial Shift

The Industrial Revolution, a period of remarkable technological and economic change, saw many individuals play vital roles. Key figures included inventors like James Watt, who improved the steam engine, and George Stephenson, who pioneered the steam locomotive, according to Britannica. Other influential figures included manufacturers like Richard Arkwright, who developed the water frame, and Michael Faraday, who made advancements in electricity.
