Where Did It All Begin? : The Industrial Shift

The Industrial Revolution started about 225 years ago. Mechanized factories based on steam engine power began opening everywhere . The steam was created by burning massive amounts of coal and the factories belched out choking clouds of smoke. About one hundred years later, machinery and transportation power systems changed. They began switching to electricity, produced mostly by burning more coal. Added to this were oil-fuelled engines. All the energy came from pre-historic sources containing carbon.

That was the beginning of our global warming problem.

Burning fossil fuels such as coal and oil releases carbon dioxide and other gases. Over the decades, the gases have billowed up into the atmosphere surrounding our planet. As the concentrations of gases got thicker, they created a kind of blanket that has caused the Earth to heat up. Other, natural cycles, have added to the global warming. The effect has been similar to that in a greenhouse, so the gases causing the warm up are called greenhouse gases (GHGs).
